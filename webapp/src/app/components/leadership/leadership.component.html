Leadership

My Fundamental Tenets
  1. Adopt a definite purpose. Stand by it until it becomes an all-consuming obsession.
  2. Embrace the struggle. It really is beautiful.
  3. There is no such thing as something for nothing.
  4. Waking up at 6am doesn't make you successful. Having the motivation to wake up and bargain with life on your own terms makes you successful.
  5. Learn the rules so well that you can break them or rewrite them.
  6. Follow well, then lead well.
  7. Set high standards for yourself and others. Apply them impartially.
  8. Remember, standards are minimum sets of criteria for what is acceptable. Always go above and beyond.
  9. Create specific objectives and specific rewards.
  10. Multi-tasking is a myth. Keep zen-like focus on one objective at a time.
How to tap the mind's full potential
  1. Realize that you are a fractal of divinity. Just as you were created, you too can and should create.
  2. Create a vision of success (however you define success)
    1. Have specific goals
    2. Have specific timelines and milestones
  3. Acquire Knowledge, especially of hard things (as much as possible on a continuous basis)
    1. Make notes and references to key concepts as you learn
    2. This build confidence, creates a template for success, encourages unique insight and cross-domain thinking
  4. Strive for balance as a living being
    1. physical - diet, exercise including daily stretching (also counts as meditation)
    2. mental - reading/learning
    3. spiritual - meditation/prayer
  5. Strive for balance as someone with purpose
    1. work - know when to work and when to relax (rest is part of the plan)
    2. life - don't neglect those important to you, but be disciplined in giving them time
  6. Allocate Time, Money, and Energy appropriately
    1. Realize you only have a finite amount of time, money, and energy to allocate each day
    2. How do you want to spend your TME? Realize that giving TME to activities and people that don't contribute to your vision of success is essentially a waste

    4. Distribute as you see fit but remember: Focus, Balance
  7. Focus on one task until completion
    1. Become aware of distractions and mitigate them
