Success

Personal reflections on events in my life that I consider to be successes.

Definition of success: (noun) the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Below I have listed some successes in approximate chronological order:

Goal: Join the Air Force

I graduated high school one semester early by attending regular classes from 8:30am-3:30pm and then completing self-paced, online courses from 4pm-9pm. Early graduation gave me the opportunity to join the United States Air Force at the age of 17. While my friends were walking the stage, I was learning how to fix multi-million dollar aircraft.

Goal: Run my own business

After separating from the Air Force, I decided to try my hand at entrepreneurship by starting a shoe and clothing company. I found suppliers for sneakers, boots, polo shirts, jeans, shorts, t-shirts, and printing materials. I then created my own t-shirt designs with photoshop, printed them, and heat-pressed them onto blank t-shirts. My custom-made t-shirts were distributed to independent clothiers across Charlotte. The radius of my shoe business extended beyond the state borders. People literally went crazy for my sneakers. I sold them in front of night clubs, simon malls, and sporting events. In the end, I sold over $250K worth of shoes out of the trunk of my car. Eventually I liquidated in favor of going to college...but at a nice profit.

Goal: Attend College

I was the first in my family to attend a 4-year university program. At the time of enrollment, I didn't know what major I wanted. I only knew that I wanted the best return on my investment. I asked the admissions counselor what the hardest program was at UNC Charlotte. She said it was electrical engineering. I enrolled thinking that if I can do the hardest program they offer, then I can everything else by default. Surely the hardest degree would pay a decent wage until I figured out what I really wanted to do in life. So far the plan has worked.
