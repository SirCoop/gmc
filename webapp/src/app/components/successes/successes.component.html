Success

Personal reflections on events in my life that I consider to be successes.

Definition of success: (noun) the accomplishment of an aim or purpose

Below I have listed some successes in approximate chronological order:

Goal: Provide for my son

My son Noah was born when I was 17. At the time, I lived with my mother who was hopelessly strung out on alcohol, prescription pills, and probably some other things. We are were penniless. I wrapped blankets in plastic and stuffed them into the broken windows sills to keep out the draft. We had no heat in the house. For three winters I used the oven to keep the house warm. I still remember waking up to freezing temperatures in the morning. I couldn't raise a child in this environment. I knew something had to be done. I wrote a letter my high school principal, Mr. Anderson. In the letter, I asked Mr. Anderson if I could be enrolled into a special program that would allow me to take extra coursework online. I stated that my living conditions were deplorable, and I needed money to provide for my son. I needed to graduate early to get my life started. Most kids in this program are behind graduation schedule and use this time to catch up. I was in it to get ahead. I went to high school during regular hours, 8:30am-3:30pm, and then I took my self-paced courses from 4pm-9pm. After six months, I graduated a semester early and shipped off to the Air Force. I owe Mr. Anderson a debt of gratitude for going to bat for me more than once. He also covered my cab fare once for a ride to school after unsuccessfully trying to ditch the cab. The end justifies the means right? The Air Force was a great experience for me.

Goal: Attend College

I was the first in my family to attend a 4-year university program. At the time of enrollment, I didn't know what major I wanted. I only knew that I wanted the best return on my investment. I asked the admissions counselor what the hardest programs was at UNC Charlotte. She said it was electrical engineering. I enrolled thinking that if I can do the hardest program they offer, then I can everything else as well. Surely the hardest degree to earn would pay a decent wage until I figured out what I really wanted to do in life. So far the plan has worked.
